The ingredients needed to make Vickys Pizza-Style Potatoes, GF DF EF SF NF:
  1. Prepare 1 1/2 tbsp olive oil, divided
  2. Make ready 4 medium baking potatoes, any variety
  3. Take 1 onion, peeled & chopped
  4. Prepare 1 courgette / zucchini, sliced
  5. Prepare 1 summer squash, peeled & sliced
  6. Take 225 grams sliced white mushrooms
  7. Get to taste salt & pepper
  8. Prepare 240 ml marinara sauce, recipe below if required*
  9. Prepare cheese of choice to top, I use 'Violife' (Vegan, Soy-Free)
  10. Prepare Marinara Sauce
  11. Prepare 400 grams can chopped tomatoes
  12. Get 6 tbsp tomato puree/paste
  13. Make ready 3 tbsp olive oil
  14. Take 2 1/2 tbsp finely diced onion
  15. Get 2 tbsp freshly chopped basil
  16. Get 50 ml white wine
  17. Make ready 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  18. Make ready 1/2 tsp salt
  19. Get 1/2 clove garlic, finely chopped
  20. Take 1 pinch pepper
  21. Take sugar
Instructions to make Vickys Pizza-Style Potatoes, GF DF EF SF NF:
  1. Preheat the oven to gas 6 / 200C / 400°F
  2. Prick the potatoes all over with a fork, brush with 1/2 a tablespoon of the oil and arrange on a baking sheet. Bake until tender, around 1 - 1 & 1/2 hours. You can also bake in the microwave on full power for 7 - 12 minutes depending on the wattage. Mine take 10 mins at 800W
  3. When the potatoes are almost tender, heat the remaining tablespoon of oil in a large frying pan over a medium high heat. Add the courgette, squash, mushrooms, onions & salt and pepper and cook, stirring occasionally, until just tender and golden brown, around 6 minutes
  4. Preheat the grill / broiler. Halve the potatoes, transfer to a baking dish cut side up and top with the vegetable mixture. Pour some warmed marinara sauce over the potatoes then top with as much cheese as you like. Grill until the cheese is melted and bubbly, 2 or 3 minutes, then transfer to plates and serve
  5. For the marinara, place the tomatoes, tomato paste, minced garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper in a blender and blitz until smooth. Saute the finely chopped onion in olive oil for 2 minutes in a large frying pan. Add the blended tomato sauce, basil and white wine. Simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
