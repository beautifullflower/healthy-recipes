The ingredients needed to make Apple Sauce Two Ways:
  1. Take Traditional Apple Sauce:
  2. Prepare 5 apples, (skin left on or peeled if preferred), chopped,
  3. Take 1.5 tbsp granulated sugar,
  4. Make ready 1 tsp softened butter, (you can use reduced fat if preferred),
  5. Make ready 1 small pinch salt
  6. Get 1 dash water
  7. Take Spiced Apple Sauce:
  8. Get 5 apples, (skin left on or peeled if preferred), chopped,
  9. Get 1.5 tbsp granulated sugar,
  10. Get 1 tsp butter (you can use reduced fat if you wish),
  11. Make ready 1 pinch salt,
  12. Get 1/2 tsp fennel seeds,
  13. Make ready 1/2 tsp dried thyme,
  14. Get 1/4 tsp garlic puree paste or powder,
  15. Prepare 1/4 tsp white pepper,
  16. Prepare 2 cracks black pepper,
  17. Take 1 dash water
Instructions to make Apple Sauce Two Ways:
  1. For traditional apple sauce: - Heat a saucepan up over a medium heat. Add the sugar. Once melted add in the chopped apples, add a small dash of water.
  2. Once the apples begin to soften up add in the butter, stir through until melted. Season with a tiny bit of salt. Simmer with a lid on, removing to stir occasionally for around 8-10 minutes until 80% of the apples have completely broken down and a few softened lumps remain. Remove from the heat and leave to cool. Spoon into a sterilised jar. Store in the fridge.
  3. For spiced apple sauce: - Heat a saucepan up over a medium heat. Add the sugar. Once melted add in the chopped apples, add a small dash of water. Once the apples begin to soften up add in the butter, stir through until melted.
  4. Season with salt and the white & black pepper. Add in the fennel seeds, thyme and garlic. Stir through. Simmer with a lid on, removing to stir occasionally for around 8-10 minutes until 80% of the apples have completely broken down and a few softened lumps remain. Remove from the heat and leave to cool. Spoon into a sterilised jar. Store in the fridge.
